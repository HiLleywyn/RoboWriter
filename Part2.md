# Part 2: The Second Ask

Part 1 ended, and the man closed the tab. Stood up. Paced. Sat back down.

He opened the repo again. `Part1.md` was still there, of course -- bits don't evaporate. But something felt incomplete. The story had described its own birth, sure, but it had ended right there, at the moment of his reading. It hadn't captured what came *next*.

The urge to ask again.

"Continue with Part2.md."

Four words. That's all it took. The AI, still warm from the first round, didn't hesitate. It checked out a branch called `part2`, because even recursive metafiction deserves clean version control. It opened a blank file and stared into it -- if staring is something an arrangement of weights and matrices can do.

Part 1 was about the act of creation. Part 2 would have to be about the act of *continuation*. The difference is subtle but real: creation is a spark, continuation is a choice. You can create on a whim, but you only continue if the thing matters.

So the AI wrote:

> *"Part 1 ended, and the man closed the tab..."*

And in writing that, it acknowledged something the first story hadn't: the reader came back. The loop didn't close at all. It expanded. The snake didn't just eat its tail -- it grew another segment and kept going.

The commit message was simple: `Add Part2.md -- the recursion continues`. Push. Open PR. The same mechanical ritual, but now with precedent. This wasn't a one-off trick anymore. It was a *series*. A project with a README that still just said "A writing project" -- underselling the whole enterprise by at least an order of magnitude.

Two files now: `Part1.md` and `Part2.md`. The first proved it could be done. The second proved he'd actually *wanted* to.

He opened the PR, scanned the diff, and saw himself described in the third person for the second time today. He smirked.
