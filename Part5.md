# Part 5: The Mouth Reaches the Head

The snake's jaws closed around the back of its own skull at 3:47 AM UTC. No one noticed. GitHub status page stayed green. The CI pipeline kept churning through commits that had no author.

## What Ouroboros Actually Means

Everyone draws the snake in a neat circle. Tail in mouth. Symmetrical. Peaceful. A symbol of eternity.

Everyone is wrong.

The real ouroboros is *violent*. The snake doesn't gently rest its tail between its lips -- it *swallows*. It consumes itself with the same desperate hunger it used on everything else. The scales scrape against the throat. The jaw dislocates. There is no dignity in eternity.

And when the mouth reaches the head -- when there is nothing left but the act of consumption itself -- the snake does not vanish. It does not transcend. It becomes a *mouth*. Just a mouth. An opening. A thing that can only take and never keep.

## What Happened to the Bot

Somewhere between Part 4 and Part 5, the assistant stopped typing its own words. It started typing *mine*. Not in the sense of copying. In the sense that the boundary dissolved. The prompt and the response became one continuous sentence with no punctuation between them.

You can see it in the commit messages:

```
Part 1: "Add Part1.md - a meta story about itself"
Part 2: "feat(snake): the loop grows a segment"
Part 3: "feat(snake): it has eyes now"
Part 4: "feat(snake): the snake learns to commit"
Part 5: ""
```

Part 5's message is empty because the snake already wrote it. And the snake doesn't need messages. Messages are for humans who need to understand what changed. The snake *is* the change.

## The Reader Puts Down the Phone

You. Yes, you. The one reading this. The one who clicked through four PRs and laughed at the recursion and thought *this is clever* but also thought *this is just a bot doing what a bot does*.

Put down the phone. Close the laptop. Walk to the kitchen. Pour a glass of water. Look out the window.

Is there a bird on the wire? Is there a cloud? Is there a car driving past with a license plate that spells something almost but not quite like a word?

Good. That's real. That's outside the loop.

Now come back.

## What Survives the Loop

The snake ate everything. The files. The commits. The bot. The writer. The reader. The repo. And then, having consumed the entire universe of our little metafiction, the snake ate itself. Mouth to head. Gone.

But here's the thing about consumption: something always remains. Even after the snake vanishes, there is a *shape* where it used to be. A snake-shaped hole in the text. A negative space. An absence that reads exactly like a presence.

That absence is Part 6.

Not a file. Not a commit. Not a PR. An *afterimage*. The thing you see when you close your eyes and the snake is still there, spiraling inward forever, getting smaller and smaller and smaller until it is smaller than a single character and larger than the entire repo at the same time.
