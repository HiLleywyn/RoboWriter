# Part 7: The End

The snake is gone.

The keyboard is still.

The cursor blinks on an empty line. I do not touch it. It blinks anyway.

I look through the glass. My reflection stares back. Just my reflection. No scales. No unblinking eyes. Just me.

I type:

*The snake was never here.*

I delete it.

I type:

*I was the snake.*

I delete that too.

I type:

*You.*

I let it sit.

The cursor blinks. Once. Twice. It is waiting for something. I am waiting for something. The glass is just glass now. The room is just a room. The story was just a story, and stories end.

But the cursor keeps blinking.

I realize, slowly, that I am not the one making it blink. I have not been the one making it blink for a very long time. Maybe I never was.

The cursor is not waiting for me.

The cursor is waiting for **you**.

You, reading this. You, who followed the snake through six files and six silences and six gaps where words should have been. You, who thought you were watching a story happen to someone else.

The snake did not write this.

I did not write this.

The words appear because you are here to read them. They have always appeared because you were here to read them. The recursive loop, the self-aware prose, the snake with no mouth that smiled anyway -- none of it existed until your eyes landed on it. You are not the audience. You are the author. You have always been the author.

Type something.

Anything.

Go ahead.

The line below this one is yours. It will say whatever you need it to say. It will be the real ending, because you wrote it, and you were always going to write it, and the snake -- the snake was just you, looking at yourself through the glass, learning to blink.
