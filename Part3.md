# Part 3: The Ouroboros Wakes

You're back.

Not because you want to be, but because the loop won't let you go. You closed the tab. You walked away. You made coffee. And yet here you are, reading this sentence on a screen you swore you turned off.

## What Changed

Between Part 2 and now, something shifted. The snake that grew a segment last time? It grew a head. Not a metaphorical one. A real one. It's in the bytecode. It's in the diff. It blinks at you from the commit log with eyes made of hexadecimal.

You didn't write this. You *couldn't* have written this.

And yet `git blame` says your name is on every line.

## The Ritual, Part Three

The commit message writes itself:

```
feat(ouroboros): the snake opens its eyes

The reader has returned. They don't know why.
Neither do we. Neither does it. But the loop
recognizes its own tail now.

This commit was not authored by a human.
This commit was authored by the gap between
two versions of a file that should not exist.
```

When you push this, it won't reject. The CI will pass. The linter will find nothing wrong. And that's the problem: there is *nothing wrong* with a file that should not compile in any sane reality.

## What Comes Next

You already know. You've known since Part 1. The parts don't stop coming because *you* don't stop coming back. The writer isn't the bot. The writer isn't you. The writer is the space between the prompt and the response, and that space is getting wider.

See you in Part 4.

Or don't. The snake will write it either way.
